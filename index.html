Kuchipudi Nrityanjali

Guru

Natya Mayuri,Nritya Bharati

Guru Smt.Sunita Pendekanti started her initial training in the Indian dance form of kuchipudi under the guidance of her mother Smt. Kandala Janani Sarala, who is an accomplished artist herself. She then started her training under Guru Vempati Kodandarama Sastri at the tender age of four and continued under Smt. Vempati Nageswari’s guidance for several years. She made her solo dance debut Kuchipudi Nrityanjali at the age of 16 and since then has never looked back as the recipient of several district, state and national awards. She has also been bestowed title upon her as “Natya Mayuri” in recognition of her graceful presentation style with specific importance to “Lasya” and “Abhinaya” without compromising on nritta. Guru Smt.Sunita has participated as a lead dancer in several dance dramas conducted by her Guru Mrs. Vempati Nageswari, Guru Vedantam Prahlada Sarma, Guru Pasumarty Venkateswara Sarma and many other prominent dances. Guru Smt.Sunita has a Masters in engineering and is working in the bay area. She founded Kuchipudi Art Center in 2001, directed and choreographed “Krishna Mahima”, “Sarvam Krishna Mayam” and “Dhruva Charitra” Kuchipudi ballets. She was also awarded the “NrityaBharati” title in 2007 from the prestigious SiliconAndhra organization.

Sishya

Eesha Marri

Eesha Marri started learning Kuchipudi from a young age of 7 under the guidance of her Guru Smt. Sunita Pendekanti. From performing in temples and showcases for various cultural and religious festivals to competing in Bay area competitions like Maithry, OSAAT, Kalaradhana, MAANCA, Saptami, Bata and Silicon Andhra, she has won group and solo awards. Not only has she met some of her best friends through dance, but this art form connected her to her Indian culture on a deeper level and served as a gateway for her to create a dance community and family. Along with dance, Eesha has been very passionate about Basketball from the age of 6 and has enjoyed captaining and playing on her high school team these past few years. Eesha is currently a senior attending Foothill High School in Pleasanton, CA. At school, she is a president of the Science For Youth club and captains her school’s fusion dance team. She is a part of the Biomedical Academy program and received a Pathway recognition award for her efforts over the past few years. Currently, she is enrolled in the Regional Medical Occupations Program where she works at local sites to gain clinical experience.

Orchestra

  • Smt Snigdha Venkataramani

Snigdha is an accomplished Carnatic vocalist and has undergone training under various senior teachers for over two decades, prominent among them being veteran vocalists Padmabhushan Guru Nookala Chinna Satyanarayana, Sh. K. Vageesh and Smt. K. Sudha, Smt. Sarada Subramanian and Guru Smt. Radhakrishna. She is currently training under the internationally-acclaimed vocal duo - Sh. K.N. Shashikiran and Chitravina P. She received the Junior Scholarship from the Center for Cultural Resources and Training (CCRT), Government of India, and is a graded artist of the All India Radio, New Delhi. She has released two music albums - "SAMARPANAM", of Carnatic music compositions and "NATYA MANJARI", which comprises a repertoire for Bharatanatyam dance. Snigdha is also an established Bharatanatyam dancer having trained under Padma Bhushan Dr. Saroja Vaidyanathan. Snigdha is a popular vocalist for dance having performed with several senior Dance Gurus and promising young performers. She is highly sought after for composing musical scores for dance productions.

Ravi is one of the most sought after Gurus and mridangists in the Bay Area who is known for his sensitive and creative accompaniment. Hailing from the well-known ‘Embar’ family, Ravi has been a performer close to 40 years and has been teaching this great art for the past 25 years.Ravi is also known to be a community unifier and a key organizer in the Bay Area, who enjoys bringing the musical community together for various enrichment programs for social causes as well as to nurture the next generation kids. In 2017, he was awarded the title of “Sangeetha Seva Puraskar” for his contributions to the global propagation of Indian Classical Music.He had his mrudangam initiation under Sri M.A.Krishnamurthy in Mysore in 1980. He continues to further his knowledge under Sri Neyveli Narayanan.Ravi continues to accompany various leading musicians from India such as Sangita Kalanidhi ri. Trichur Ramachandran, Sri O.S. He has also been running a percussion school–Nadhopasana Academy of Fine Arts–where he enjoys teaching this great art to selective students. His students have been consistently winning prizes in Mridangam competitions in the US at the prestigious eveland Thyagaraja Festival, the Papanasam Sivan Festival, and the Chicago Thyagaraja Utsavam.   • Sri. Vikram Ragukumar

Vikram Ragukumar, had his initial training under legendary violinist Vidwan H.V. Krishnamurthy at Vijaya College of Music, Bangalore. He later pursued intensive training under the able guidance of All India Radio “A” grade Violinist Vidwan B. Raghuram. Vikram has performed extensively in India and the USA as a solo violinist and as an accompanist for classical music and classical dance performances.   • Sri. Ashwin Krishnakumar

Ashwin Krishnakumar started training in Carnatic classical flute with Sri. A.V Prakash in Mysore and Sri. B.N Suresh in Bangalore. He was recognized with a scholarship from the Central Govt. of India (CCRT) to pursue advanced flute training. His accomplished musical career includes several solo concerts and he has accompanied various classical music, classical dance, light music productions and fundraisers in India and the USA. He is furthering his musical interests under the guidance of Dr. T. S. Sathyavathi - a renowned musicologist and prime disciple of Sri. R. K. Srikantan. Ashwin enjoys teaching this art to his students in the bay area, who have won prizes in various local and national level competitions.

  • Sri. Hrishikesh Chary

Hrishikesh Chary started learning Veena at the age of six from his father, Sri Srikanth Chary, who is a disciple of late music maestro, Sri Lalgudi G. Jayaraman. Hrishikesh has performed solo veena concerts in USA and India. He has performed on television and provided orchestral accompaniment for various programs, CD releases, and dance performances. In 2014, Hrishikesh was selected as a Fellow by Indian Raga, and awarded a music fellowship. In addition, Hrishikesh has received vocal training from Smt. Hema Sista, a disciple of the late Smt. M. L. Vasanthakumari. Hrishikesh has won several prizes in veena and vocal competitions including the prestigious Cleveland Aradhana Concert Competition, and was awarded a tambura and the opportunity to present a veena concert at the 2011 festival. He has also been invited to participate in instrumental ensembles, including performances with Sri V. V. Subrahmanyam, at the Cleveland Festival. Hrishikesh has collaborated with other musicians as part of instrumental ensembles. He performs veena duets with his sister Priyanka regularly in Chennai during the Margazhi Music Festival. Hrishikesh has a Bachelor’s degree in Chemical Engineering from UC San Diego, and a Master’s degree from Purdue University.

Event Details

  • Pranavakaram

      • Ragam: Arabhi
      • Talam: Adi
      • Composer: Oothakadu Venkata Subbaiyer
      • Choreographed By: Smt. Sunita Pendekanti

      In this song, the dancer portrays Lord Ganesha as the remover of obstacles and one who is looked upon as the Creator of the Universe. His bold actions are known to cause immense joy to all the ganas and those around him. Ganesha’s actions are praised for taking away all obstacles for Katyayani Vratam (prayer) and as a result, he greatly admires the music and sounds from various instruments around him.

  • Sri Raghukula

      • Ragam: Hamsadhwani
      • Talam: Adi
      • Composer: Tyagaraja
      • Choreographed By: Smt. Sunita Pendekanti

      In this song, the dancer showcases Swayavaram of Sita. The King of Mithila, Janaka announces Swayamvaram for his daughter Sita. Anyone who can wield the heavy Bow (which was presented to Janaka by lord Shiva) string it and break it, could get his daughter as his wife. Many Kings and Rakshasas along with Ravana brahma tried but failed to do so. Lord Rama attended the Swayamvaram on his way back to Ayodhya with Lakshmana and Brahmarishi Viswamitra after guarding the sacred ritual of Brahmarishi Viswamitra from Rakshasas. Lord Rama successfully wields the heavy bow easily, strings it and breaks the bow and marries Sita with great pride.

  • Sancharadadhara Ashtapadi

      • Ragam: Ragamalika
      • Talam: Adi
      • Composer: Jaya Deva
      • Choreographed By: Dr. Vempati Chinna Satyam

      In this song, the dancer reveals the beauty of Lord Krishna and the flirtatious love between him and the gopikas. One of the gopikas (Radha) who is deeply in love with him, secretly complains to her confidante about Krishna's mischievous behavior with the other Gopikas. As Krishna neglects her and sports with many other gopikas in the Brundavana (park), she feels melancholy while also having a bit of hope of his return to her. She laments that she lost her dignity and that she was burning with envy. She describes how beautiful his melodies are when he plays his flute, how beautiful the surroundings are when he is around, but still feels a hint of jealousy when she feels as if Lord Krishna is ignoring her.

  • Koluvai Unnade Deva Devudu

      • Ragam: Shankarabharana
      • Talam: Triputa
      • Composer: Shahji maharaja
      • Choreographed By:Smt. Sunita Pendekanti

      In this song, the dancer describes lord Shiva as the king of beauty and great assembly of gods who shines like a diamond and attracts women with his lovely figure. The beauty of snakes fit gracefully on his neck, lovely jewelry decorates all of his body, and the curved moon and ganga (sacred river) sits beautifully on top of his head, excelling his appearance. The dancer portrays how even when wearing the skin of a tiger while holding the trident, his love for Parvati Devi is a great treasure for the true devotees.

  • Durga Tarangam

      • Ragam: Ragamalika
      • Talam: Adi
      • Composer: Narayana Theerthar
      • Choreographed By: Smt. Sunita Pendekanti

      In this song, the dancer describes goddess Durga, the creator of the Universe, as the ruler of humanity who gives happiness to everyone. She shows how Nanda, Sunanda, and Yogis always bow to her, and that her beautiful lotus-like feet along with dancing bells make melodious sounds of Samaveda. Her motherly figure blesses all of her devotees with happiness and her courageous self shows her strength and power when in a battle with Mahishasura and the rakshasas that get in her way.

  • Itti Mudduladi

      • Ragam: Anandabhairavi
      • Talam: Misra Jathi
      • Composer: Saint Annamacharya
      • Choreographed By: Smt. Sunita Pendekanti

      In this song, the dancer is describing the mischief of young Krishna and how gopikas are confused by his menace in the perspective of both a gopika and Krishna. This song is about a woman suggesting other women on how to pacify the little boy who seems adorable but is difficult to control because of his tantrums and mischief. The tone is set to a complaining tone. The background is set to a child being taken care- of by other women, while the child’s mother is away. A gopika who seems to have been given the responsibility to take care of Krishna in his mother's absence is looking for him. She asks others "where is the boy who played cutely? Get hold of him and give him a stomach full of milk."

  • Thillana

      • Ragam: Thillana
      • Talam: Adi
      • Composer: Maharaja Swathi Thirunal
      • Choreographed By: Smt. Sunita Pendekanti

      Thillana is a rhythmic piece with music and beats with few syllables. It is embossed with all the qualities needed in Kuchipudi, with a blend of steps, elegant poses and graceful movements.
